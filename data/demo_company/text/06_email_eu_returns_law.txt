Camille, team —

Quick legal note that needs to be reflected in the returns policy.

Under EU Directive 2011/83/EU (Consumer Rights), customers in any EU member state have an automatic 14-day "withdrawal period" for digital products purchased online. They do not need to provide a reason and we cannot require one.

This applies to:
- Our Trail Box subscription (digital newsletter + planning tool component)
- Any future digital-only SKU we add (route-finder app, etc.)

This does NOT apply to:
- Physical product returns from the EU — those still follow our standard 30-day policy in the v2.1 returns document
- Custom embroidered orders (those are exempt under Article 16(c))
- Any product where the seal has been broken on a hygiene item (Article 16(e))

Effective immediately:
- EU customers identified by Stripe billing country requesting refund within 14 days of purchase on the Trail Box subscription → automatic full refund, no approval needed.
- Beyond 14 days → revert to whatever the standard policy says.

This SUPERSEDES section 4.2 of the Returns Policy v2.1 (which says all subscriptions are non-refundable after 7 days). The 7-day rule remains correct for non-EU customers on subscriptions.

I'm working with Camille to publish a v2.2 of the returns doc that has both rules side by side. In the meantime — please follow this email.

If anyone is unsure whether a customer counts as "EU," check the billing country in Stripe (NOT the shipping address — billing is what matters for consumer rights jurisdiction).
